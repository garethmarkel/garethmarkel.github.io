title: "Nature, Nurture, and Socioeconomic Outcomes: New Evidence from Sib Pairs and Molecular Genetic Data"

We all know two sibling share about half of thier genotype. What is less common is the exact degree of genetic relatedness of two non-identical twin siblings, is randomly assigned--it's an almost perfect natural experiment. This paper uses genotype data on over 1 million participants, hailing from 6 countries and 2 continents, to identify 80,000 sibling pairs, calculate the amount of DNA actually shared by each pair, and use that quantity to identify the proportion of variance in an outcome that is caused by genes instead of environment. We find evidence of sizeable genetic influences on many outcomes.

This paper is currently accepted and forthcoming in *American Economic Review: Insights*. AEA members can preview it [here](https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/aeri.20250281&&from=f), and the working paper can be found [on SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5225447).
